My side projects - Deep Learning

When George Hotz of Comma.ai presented the Comma One at Techcrunch Disrupt, I was immediately hooked to the very idea of an autonomous car. Hotz spoke of autonomous driving not as a technological advancement, but as a problemto solve. A very difficult problem where a wrong solution could jeopardise safety of human lives. Driven by enthusiasm I started reading up on research papers and public repositories on the topic, trying to figure out the details in this cutting edge.

Although I am still not satisfied with my level of understanding of these increasingly complex systems, it has enabled me to step into the world of AI and deep learning. I started with basic MNIST predictions to more advanced datasets like GTSRB using CNNs. There are multiple theories that go into the running of even a simple CNN: batch normalization, dropout and other regularization methods, softmax, data augmentation, etc.

Currently, I am teaching myself the basics of Semantic Segmentation and SegNets. I'll be updating my journey on my github page soon
